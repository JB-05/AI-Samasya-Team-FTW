- Report must be narrative, not bullet-heavy
- No section titles implying evaluation or scoring
- No comparison to norms or peers
- No timelines or progress metrics
- Disclaimer must appear as final sentence
- Use paragraph form, not lists
- Avoid categorical statements
- Use observational language ("shows", "appears", "suggests")
- No imperative commands ("must", "should", "need to")
- No definitive conclusions
- Focus on what was observed, not what it means
- Supportive suggestions only, not prescriptions
- No urgency or alarm language
- No labels or categories
